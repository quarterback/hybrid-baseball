# PRD: Hybrid Baseball-Cricket-Pesäpallo Simulation (v0.2)

**Working name:** TBD
**Owner:** Ron Bronson
**Status:** Draft for build

## 1. Concept

A baseball simulation with cricket-style innings structure and a pesäpallo-flavored stay-at-plate mechanic. Otherwise standard baseball.

## 2. Rules specification

### 2.1 Game structure

- **One inning per game**, two halves: top (visitors bat) and bottom (home bats).
- Between halves: **halftime**.
- Each half-inning ends when EITHER:
  - 27 outs accumulated, OR
  - Entire lineup has batted through and final batter is dismissed
- Higher run total at end of bottom half wins.
- Ties allowed → super-inning tiebreaker.

### 2.2 Super-inning tiebreaker

- Each manager picks 5 batters from the full roster (any batter, including those dismissed in regulation).
- That side bats until all 5 are dismissed; record runs.
- Other side does the same.
- Higher super-inning total wins.
- Repeat with new 5-batter selections until decided.
- Any pitcher may be used in a super-inning.

### 2.3 Pitching and counts

Standard baseball. Balls, strikes, walks, strikeouts, HBP, foul rules, foul tip caught = out. Pitcher substitution standard. No forced rotation. Track pitcher fatigue as batters faced in current spell. No hard cap on spell length — fatigue-driven.

### 2.4 Lineup composition

- 12 batters per side: 9 position players (pitcher must bat) + 3 jokers (designated hitters).
- Jokers cannot play the field. Jokers can be swapped in for any fielder (the fielder leaves the game).
- **Joker insertion is flexible (pesäpallo-style):** jokers do not have a fixed lineup slot. A manager can insert a joker at any point in the batting order during the inning. Constraint: each individual joker can only bat once per half-inning.
- Once a batter is dismissed, they are out for the half-inning. They do not return.
- Pinch hitters can be inserted mid-inning, standard baseball — pinch hitter takes the replaced batter's spot, replaced batter is gone.

### 2.5 The stay mechanic

When a batter makes fair contact AND at least one runner is on base, batter chooses at contact:

**Run:** Standard baseball. Batter becomes batter-runner, all standard plays apply. At-bat ends.

**Stay:** Batter does not leave home plate. Defense plays runners only. Batter cannot be put out on this play. No force at first, no double play through first. At-bat continues with fresh 0-0 count.

### 2.6 Stay constraints

- Bases empty: stay unavailable.
- Two-strike contact + stay: batter is out, runners advance per fielded play.
- Caught fly ball with stay: batter is out. Runners may tag.
- Infield fly rule: standard.
- Bunt + stay: allowed, recorded as a hit.
- Stay with runner thrown out: that runner is out, at-bat continues with fresh count.
- Multiple stays per at-bat: allowed, no cap.
- Home runs: stay does not apply. Batter runs and scores normally.

### 2.7 Hit credit

A batter can record multiple hits in a single at-bat via repeated stays. Each stay that results in runner advancement without an out on the batter = one hit credited. Hits from stays are uncategorized — they are simply hits. Singles, doubles, triples, and home runs only exist when the batter actually runs and reaches a base.

### 2.8 Stay choice mechanics

Decision is made at contact, not pre-pitch. Defense does not know in advance. Sim models this probabilistically based on batter archetype, contact quality, base state, outs, score, half-inning context.

### 2.9 Everything else

Standard baseball: stolen bases, pickoffs, balks, wild pitches, passed balls, sacrifice bunts (likely obsolete given stay — track usage), intentional walks, defensive shifts.

## 3. Statistics

Standard baseball stats plus:

- **Stays:** count per batter / game / team
- **Stay RBI:** runs driven in via stay
- **Stay efficiency:** runs scored per stay attempt
- **Multi-hit at-bats:** at-bats producing multiple credited hits
- **Partnership runs:** runs between two consecutive outs
- **Spell length:** consecutive batters faced by a pitcher

Stay hits are recorded as hits, no extra-base subdivision.

## 4. Simulation requirements

### 4.1 Architecture

- Python game-state engine.
- Jinja2 templates for play-by-play rendering.
- State persists in Python; Jinja renders snapshots.
- No GUI in v1. Text output only.

### 4.2 Core state

```
GameState:
  half: "top" | "bottom" | "super"
  super_inning_number: int (0 if not in super)
  batting_team, fielding_team
  outs: int (0–27, or 0–5 in super)
  bases: {first, second, third} → batter_id or None
  current_batter_id, current_pitcher_id
  count: {balls: 0–3, strikes: 0–2}
  score: {team_a, team_b}
  partnership_runs: int
  pitcher_spell_count: int
  lineup_position: int per team
  dismissed_batters: set per team
  active_lineup: list of 12 (or 5 in super)
  jokers_used_this_half: set per team
  jokers_available: set per team
```

### 4.3 Event types

- `ball`, `called_strike`, `swinging_strike`, `foul`, `foul_tip_caught`, `hit_by_pitch`, `wild_pitch`, `passed_ball`
- `ball_in_play` → `contact_resolved`:
  - `run_chosen` → standard play
  - `stay_chosen` → modified play
- `stolen_base_attempt`, `pickoff_attempt`, `balk`
- `joker_insertion` (manager event, mid-inning)
- `pinch_hit` (manager event)
- `pitching_change` (manager event)

### 4.4 Probability models

- Pitch outcome given pitcher skill, batter skill, count, fatigue.
- Contact quality given swing and pitch quality.
- Stay-vs-run choice given contact quality, base state, outs, score, half.
- Fielding outcome given contact quality and positioning.
- Baserunning outcome given runner speed and fielding play.
- Manager decisions for joker insertion, pinch hitting, pitching changes (heuristic v1).

Rule-based heuristics acceptable for v1.

### 4.5 Stay choice heuristic (v1)

- Stay if: weak/medium contact AND runners in scoring position AND <2 outs AND not 2-strike count.
- Run if: hard contact, OR 2 outs, OR bases empty (forced), OR ball clearly leaving the park.
- Tunable per batter: `stay_aggressiveness` (0.0–1.0), `contact_quality_threshold`.

### 4.6 Joker insertion heuristic (v1)

- Insert highest-skill available joker when: runners in scoring position AND current scheduled batter is a weak hitter (e.g., pitcher) AND game leverage is high.
- Constraint: each joker once per half-inning.

### 4.7 Output

Jinja templates render pitch-level events, at-bat outcomes, half-inning summaries, halftime summary, partnership log, spell log, box score, line score, super-inning log.

## 5. Scope for v1

**In scope:**
- Single game, two AI teams, seeded determinism.
- One-inning structure with halftime and super-inning tiebreaker.
- 12-batter lineups with flexible joker insertion.
- Stay mechanic with multi-hit at-bats.
- Heuristic decision models.
- Text output via Jinja.
- Box score, partnership log, spell log.

**Out of scope:**
- GUI / web frontend.
- Season simulation.
- Roster construction tools.
- ML decision policies.
- Real player names / stats import.
- Live human manager input.

## 6. Build phases

**Phase 1:** State machine + event resolver, raw state output. Verify rules with scripted scenarios including: 27-out half, full-lineup-out half, multi-hit at-bats, joker insertion, super-inning, halftime transition.

**Phase 2:** Probability models and manager decision heuristics. Full-game end-to-end with seeded randomness.

**Phase 3:** Jinja templates for play-by-play.

**Phase 4:** Box score, partnership log, spell log, super-inning log, halftime summary.

**Phase 5:** Tuning. Play 100 games, measure stay rate, half-inning duration, run distribution, super-inning frequency. Adjust heuristics.
