## On ERA

Two real options, both defensible:

**Option A: Keep the name "ERA," redefine the math.** Earned runs allowed per 27 outs faced. Same denominator swap as everything else. League-average ERA in O27 will land somewhere around 11.50 given the ~24 R/G environment, which is a wildly different number from MLB's 4.00 — but the *shape* of the stat is identical. A pitcher with an ERA below league average is still good; one above is still bad. Sortable, comparable across pitchers, instantly readable to anyone with a baseball brain.

The downside: a 9.20 ERA looks horrific to anyone seeing it without context. They'll think every pitcher is bad. The "ERA league average is ~11.50" disclaimer has to live somewhere, probably on the leaderboard page or as a footnote.

**Option B: Rename it to RA27 (Runs Allowed per 27).** Same math as Option A, different name. Removes the cognitive trap of seeing "9.20 ERA" and reading it through MLB instincts. Honest about the fact that O27 is a different sport with a different scale.

The downside: it's a new term to learn, and "ERA" is so deeply baseball-coded that breaking from it feels precious. Strat-O-Matic and OOTP both keep ERA even when simulating very different run environments.

**My recommendation:** keep **ERA**, redefine the math. Add a small "(O27 league avg: 11.50)" reference somewhere on the pitcher leaderboard page so the number has context. The familiarity wins. Same logic for **WHIP** (walks + hits per 27 outs faced) and **K/9, BB/9, HR/9** — keep the names, swap the denominator. The "/9" is a baseball idiom by now, not a literal "per 9 innings" claim.

The one place to be careful: when comparing O27 stats to MLB stats, the numbers are not interchangeable. A 4.00 ERA in O27 is a Hall of Fame season; a 4.00 ERA in MLB is a journeyman. Anyone porting their baseball intuition needs to recalibrate. The leaderboard page footnote does that work.

## What this means for FIP, WHIP, OPS, etc.

- **FIP** — keep the name, recalibrate the constants. Same trick.
- **WHIP** — keep the name, swap denominator.
- **OPS, OBP, SLG** — these don't have a denominator-swap problem. They're rate stats based on PAs. They work in O27 unchanged.
- **AVG** — works unchanged.
- **K/9, BB/9, HR/9** — keep, swap denominator silently.
- **WAR** — recalibrate replacement level for the O27 environment, keep the name.

## On the pitcher endurance metric

Want to call this something. Options:

- **AVG OUT REACHED** — average out number of the pitcher's last batter. A workhorse averages 22; a committee piece averages 8.
- **DEEPEST OUT** — single-game best (highest out number reached in a game).
- **OS% per appearance** — already on the table, rolled up at the season level becomes a workload total.

I'd surface **average out reached** as a sortable column. It tells the workhorse-vs-committee story directly without needing a curve. Pair it with appearances and total outs recorded for the season-level workload picture.

So the pitcher line becomes: `BF · OS% · AOR (avg out reached) · ERA · WHIP · K · BB · HR · K/9 · BB/9`. Nine columns, all baseball-coded except OS% and AOR, both of which describe O27's actual structure.
