Stats methodology has real problems. Five things, in priority order:

1. FIP IS BROKEN. League-average FIP should equal league-average 
   ERA (~11.50). The leaderboard shows FIP of 0.54, 1.35, 3.04 at 
   the top — these would imply pitchers 10+ runs better than league 
   average, which is impossible. The FIP recalibration from Task #32 
   either wasn't done or produced wrong constants. Re-derive the 
   FIP constant against the actual O27 run environment so 
   league-average FIP = league-average ERA.

2. WIN COUNT IS IMPOSSIBLE. Il-Ho Park leads with 41 wins. He has 
   59 pitching appearances. A pitcher cannot win more games than 
   he appeared in. Either wins are being double-counted, or "W" 
   means something other than pitching wins (team record when he 
   played?). Audit the W calculation. Real wins are awarded to 
   one pitcher per game per the standard rule.

3. STAYS IS A LEADERBOARD CATEGORY AGAIN. Task #32 said drop STY 
   everywhere. Remove the "Stays" leaderboard. The H/AB column 
   surfaces the same information as a real baseball stat.

   PATCH ROW. Outs from caught stealing, fielder's choice secondary 
   outs, and other non-batter-attributed outs should be properly 
   attributed in the engine — either to the batter whose PA caused 
   them, to the runner thrown out, or to a team-level "other outs" 
   that shows up cleanly in the totals. The "legacy" patch is a 
   hack. Fix the attribution logic in the engine, not the totals row.

   games. A workhorse facing 56 batters in one game is implausible. 
   These are likely doubleheaders being merged from the schedule 
   bug, or super-innings being appended to regulation lines.
