Methodology
Batting rates are PA-denominated. PAVG = H/PA; OPS+ / wOBA+ are league-relativized (100 = league avg, higher = better). 2C (Second-Chance ABs) counts contact events where the batter chose to stay (and earned a hit while spending a strike from their AB-budget) instead of run. The load-bearing O27 mechanic. Listed as a counting stat — the underlying rate is 1-3% of PAs league-wide, too small to read as a percentage.

Pitching uses three result-tier metrics that each tell a different story. wERA weights earned runs by arc position (outs 1–9 ×0.85, 10–18 ×1.00, 19–27 ×1.20) and league-anchors to raw ER/27. xRA (Expected Runs Allowed) is a non-negative linear-weights formula — coefficients HR ≈ 1.4, single ≈ 0.45, BB / HBP ≈ 0.32, K / out = 0 — multiplicatively anchored so league xRA = league wERA. Replaces the previous xFIP column, which produced negative values for high-K small-sample pitchers because the FIP coefficient sign convention. Decay measures late-arc fade (K%_arc1 − K%_arc3 in points; lower = more durable). GSc is a per-appearance Game Score (50 = neutral, 100 = perfection). OS+ is league-relative outs per appearance (100 = avg). PA-rate K% counts foul-outs as Ks. WAR uses an O27-fitted runs-per-win factor (~21 vs MLB's ~10). W/L credits an SP with ≥ 12 outs and the lead, otherwise the most-effective reliever.
